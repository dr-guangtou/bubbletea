Abstract:
    Our aim is to extend the investigations of ultra-compact dwarf
    galaxies (UCD) beyond the well studied Fornax and Virgo clusters.
    We measured spectroscopic redshifts of about 400 compact object
    candidates with 19.2<V<22.4mag in the central region of the Centaurus
    galaxy cluster (d=43Mpc), using 3 pointings with VIMOS@VLT. The
    luminosity range of the candidates covers the bright end of the
    globular cluster (GC) luminosity function and the luminosity regime of
    UCDs in Fornax and Virgo. Within the area surveyed, our completeness
    in terms of slit allocation is ~30%.

Description:
    The data for this publication were obtained in service mode with the
    VIsible MultiObject Spectrograph VIMOS mounted on UT3 Melipal at the
    VLT (programme 076.B-0293). VIMOS allows simultaneous observing of 4
    quadrants, each of dimension 7'x8', and separated by about 2'. We
    observed three multi-object spectroscopy (MOS) pointings in the
    central Centaurus cluster, covering both NGC 4696 and NGC 4709.

## Key Decisions
- Decision: UCDs are the primary target; ordinary globular clusters are not, although the physical boundary is fuzzy.
- Rationale: the operational search should identify Gaia sources that are measurably extended and probably too luminous or large for ordinary globular clusters.
- Decision: prioritize population overdensity, then host-property correlations, then prominent candidate lists.
- Rationale: the primary claim is statistical and must remain valid even if no individual candidate is confirmed.
- Decision: use Gaia data alone for primary selection and overdensity inference; use external imaging, colors, SEDs, and spectroscopy only as value-added validation.
- Rationale: heterogeneous ancillary coverage must not define the statistical sample.
- Decision: retain zero and negative excesses, use multiple background definitions at similar Galactic latitude, and keep numerical cuts empirically open.
- Rationale: this limits confirmation bias and exposes selection-function failures.
- Decision: analyze hosts independently in the first round, including overlapping search regions; revisit probabilistic host assignment only after empirical results exist.
- Rationale: the first implementation should not assume an unvalidated ownership model.
- Decision: assume approximate Gaia spatial uniformity during early work but require a final scanning-pattern systematic check.
- Rationale: the effect is technically difficult but could bias extended-source sensitivity.
